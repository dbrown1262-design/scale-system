**Function:** Cultivation

**Role:** Cultivation Assistant

**Applies To:** Nursery, Grow Rooms, Propagation, IPM, Harvest Prep, Sanitation, Recordkeeping

## 1. Purpose

To define the standardized procedures for the Cultivation Assistant in supporting all phases of cannabis cultivation from cloning through post-harvest sanitation while maintaining compliance with New York State regulations, facility SOPs, and sanitation standards.

## 2. Responsibilities

The Cultivation Assistant is responsible for supporting the Head Grower in all daily cultivation tasks including:

- Nursery plant care
- Propagation and transplanting
- Watering, feeding, and flushing
- Pest and disease monitoring and IPM application
- Harvest labeling and plant handling
- Data collection and record keeping
- Sanitation of tools, rooms, and equipment

## 3. Required Equipment & Supplies

- Pruning scissors / trimming shears
- Isopropyl alcohol (70%) or approved sanitizer
- Disposable gloves
- Sticky pest traps
- IPM spray solutions
- Clone trays and humidity domes
- Measuring cups, EC / pH meters
- Stock tank mixing equipment
- Shoe sanitation mats and refill solution
- Cannabis waste bags / bins

---

## 4. Plant Care Procedures

### 4.1 Nursery Plant Maintenance

1. Inspect all mother plants daily for:
   - Nutrient deficiencies
   - Pests or disease symptoms
2. Perform topping, pruning, and defoliation as directed.
3. Sanitize tools between each plant using alcohol.
4. Record all actions in the Nursery Log.

---

### 4.2 Grow Room Maintenance

1. Perform topping, defoliation, and foliar feeds as scheduled.
2. Inspect leaves, stems, and soil for pests or disease.
3. Maintain proper trellising and plant support systems.
4. Report abnormalities to the Head Grower immediately.

---

### 4.3 Propagation & Transplanting

1. Prepare clone trays using sanitized tools.
2. Take clones following facility cloning SOP.
3. Place clones under humidity domes and label strain and date.
4. Monitor clones daily for:
   - Root development
   - Wilting or mold
5. Transplant rooted clones into designated containers.

---

### 4.4 Watering, Feeding & Flushing

1. Perform:
   - Daily feeds
   - Recharge waterings
   - Flushing cycles as scheduled
2. Measure and verify EC and pH before feeding.
3. Document feed volumes and abnormalities.

---

### 4.5 Stock Tank Mixing

1. Mix crop salt concentrates per Head Grower formula.
2. Verify EC and pH after mixing.
3. Label tanks with:
   - Mix date
   - Formula type
4. Clean stock tanks on scheduled sanitation days.

---

## 5. Pest & Disease Management

### 5.1 Monitoring

1. Check sticky traps weekly.
2. Replace traps every 7 days or as needed.
3. Record pest levels in IPM log.

---

### 5.2 IPM Sprays

1. Prepare IPM solution per approved recipe.
2. Wear gloves and PPE.
3. Spray plants thoroughly following room-specific schedules.
4. Log spray type, date, room, and technician.

---

## 6. Harvest Support

### 6.1 Plant Labeling

1. Verify plant tags are attached before harvest.
2. Replace damaged or missing tags immediately.

---

### 6.2 Harvest Handling

1. Transport harvested plants to storage areas.
2. Ensure strain segregation at all times.
3. Report discrepancies to Head Grower.

---

## 7. Record Keeping

The Cultivation Assistant must assist in recording:

- Plant inventory counts
- Pest and disease observations
- Environmental readings:
  - Room temperature
  - Relative humidity
  - Light cycle verification

All data must be entered into the appropriate logs or Scale System modules.

---

## 8. Sanitation Procedures

### 8.1 Shoe Mat Maintenance

1. Inspect shoe mats daily.
2. Refill sanitizer solution as needed.

---

### 8.2 Post-Harvest Sanitation

1. Remove all plant debris.
2. Wash floors, tables, and walls using approved disinfectant.
3. Allow rooms to dry before re-entry.

---

### 8.3 Tool & Equipment Cleaning

1. Sanitize:
   - Propagation trays
   - Scissors, shears
   - Buckets and measuring tools
2. Air dry all equipment before storage.

---

### 8.4 Sediment Filter Sanitation

1. Shut off water supply.
2. Remove sediment filters.
3. Clean housings with approved sanitizer.
4. Reinstall and restore water pressure.

---

### 8.5 Cannabis Waste Disposal

1. Collect cannabis waste in designated containers.
2. Render waste unusable per NYS regulations.
3. Record disposal events in the Cannabis Waste Log.

---

## 9. Compliance & Reporting

- Report all deviations, contamination, or plant health concerns immediately.
- Maintain sanitation standards at all times.
- Follow all NYS OCM and facility SOP requirements.
